flight 2325 was being operated by horizon air as a regularly scheduled 14cfr121 passenger flight and had departed seattle washington approximately 0650 destined for portland .
visual meteorological conditions existed and an ifr flight plan was in effect .
post-incident examination revealed that the number five compressor bearing was destroyed as well as associated air seal .
the three crew and 30 passengers were uninjured .
the passengers were bussed to the terminal .
the crew and passengers then evacuated through the forward left main cabin exit onto the runway .
there was evidence of oil leakage and fire within the breather case and compressor section and the breather tube had melted free allowing hot air to escape into the engine compartment .
the pilot declared an emergency and during the rollout the engine fire warning indicator illuminated and the engine was shut down .
any errors in this report will be corrected when the final report has been completed .
the pt120a engine has been removed from the aircraft and quarantined awaiting coordination with pratt & whitney for further examination and disassembly .
this is preliminary information subject to change and may contain errors .
